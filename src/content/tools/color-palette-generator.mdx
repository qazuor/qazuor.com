## ğŸ¨ Color Theory Basics

### Harmony Rules

**Complementary**: Colors opposite on the color wheel
- High contrast and vibrant
- Great for call-to-action elements
- Use one as dominant, other as accent

**Triadic**: Three colors evenly spaced on color wheel
- Vibrant and balanced
- Choose one primary, two accent colors
- Great for playful designs

**Analogous**: Colors next to each other on wheel
- Harmonious and pleasing
- Found often in nature
- Perfect for calming designs

**Split-Complementary**: Base color + two adjacent to complement
- High contrast but more subtle than complementary
- Good balance of harmony and contrast

### Psychology of Colors

- **Red**: Energy, passion, urgency
- **Blue**: Trust, calm, professional
- **Green**: Nature, growth, harmony
- **Yellow**: Happiness, creativity, attention
- **Purple**: Luxury, creativity, mystery
- **Orange**: Enthusiasm, warmth, caution
- **Black**: Elegance, sophistication, power
- **White**: Purity, simplicity, cleanliness

## ğŸ’¡ Design Tips

### Brand Colors
- Start with your primary brand color
- Generate complementary palette
- Test across different materials and screens
- Consider cultural color meanings

### Web Design
- Ensure sufficient contrast for accessibility
- Use 60-30-10 rule: 60% dominant, 30% secondary, 10% accent
- Test on different devices and screen types
- Consider dark mode variations

### Print Design
- Convert to CMYK before printing
- Account for color shifts between screen and print
- Test with actual printing materials
- Consider paper color and texture

## ğŸ”§ Accessibility Guidelines

### WCAG Contrast Ratios
- **Normal text**: 4.5:1 minimum
- **Large text**: 3:1 minimum
- **UI elements**: 3:1 minimum
- **AAA rating**: 7:1 for normal text

### Color Blindness Considerations
- Don't rely solely on color to convey information
- Use patterns, shapes, or text labels
- Test with color blindness simulators
- Common types: Deuteranopia, Protanopia, Tritanopia
